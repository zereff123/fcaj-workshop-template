# Summary Report: “AWS Study Tour: Enterprise Cloud Architectures and Industry Application”

### Event Objectives
- Experience real-world cloud computing and modern application architectures in an enterprise environment.
- Update on recruitment trends, market demands, and the overall landscape of the Cloud, Data, and AI industry.
- Create networking opportunities and learn the mindset of senior engineers and experts currently working at AWS and its partners.
- Help students reshape their learning paths to meet the strict standards of today's tech industry.

### List of Speakers
- **Mr. Nguyen Gia Hung** - Head of Solution Architecture at AWS Vietnam (Founder of First Cloud AI Journey).
- **Mr. Banh Cam Vinh** - Data Engineer at Renova Cloud (AWS Community Builder).
- **Ms. Nhu Tran** - Account Manager at AWS Vietnam.
- **Mr. Khang Nguyen** - Solution Architect at Cloud Kinetics (Swinburne Alumnus).

### Key Highlights

#### 1. The Cloud & AI Job Market Landscape (Mr. Nguyen Gia Hung)
- **Strict market:** Companies hiring Interns now require Junior-level knowledge (like Kubernetes, K8s, AI). However, the total number of jobs has not decreased; the demand has just shifted to the Senior level.
- **Industry-based selection:** Instead of just choosing a role (Data, DevOps...), engineers need to solve problems for 6 core industries (Finance & Banking, Retail, Telecommunications, Manufacturing, etc.).
- **Formula for promotion:** Results = Capability × Visibility × Consistency. CVs are currently screened by AI, so it is crucial to demonstrate a "Lifelong learning" spirit through certificates, public side-projects, and tech blogs.

#### 2. Data Architecture and Data Engineering Reality (Mr. Banh Cam Vinh)
- **Difference between school and work:** School projects usually have clean data and low risk. In reality, data comes from various messy sources, business requirements change constantly, and the consequences of errors directly affect the company's revenue.
- **One Framework Mindset:** There is always a standard DNA for data platforms (Ingestion, Processing, Storage, Governance, Analytics). Tools may change, but the architectural mindset remains constant.
- **Communication skills:** Communication is a "technical skill". Business understanding helps Data Engineers identify the right problems instead of just blindly writing code.

#### 3. Overcoming Fears and Seizing Opportunities (Ms. Nhu Tran)
- **Identifying fears:** Students often fear judgment and making mistakes due to the habit of optimizing for correctness in school. The best way to overcome this is to face it directly and treat public speaking as a psychological internship.
- **Creating Visibility through Small Talk:** Don't avoid your boss or lecturers. Small conversations (asking about their weekend, offering support) are keys to making them remember you.
- **Finding the Blue Ocean:** The best opportunities are often not listed on public job boards. They lie in actively building relationships and planting seeds of value early on.

#### 4. Learning Mindset in the AI Era (Mr. Khang Nguyen)
- **Using AI properly:** "You can outsource your thinking, but you cannot outsource your understanding" - You can use AI to complete assignments faster, but you cannot outsource your foundational understanding to it. AI only amplifies what you already grasp.
- **School is paying to make mistakes:** Make the most of the university environment to ask "Why?". Master fundamental subjects (Networking, Security) as they are the foundation for going far in the Cloud industry.
- **Experience vs. Years of Experience:** In your first jobs, choose roles that offer rich experiences, extensive networking, and room for growth rather than solely focusing on the starting salary.

### What I Learned

#### Career Path Design Mindset
- You can no longer hide in a room coding and waiting to become successful. Good engineers must step out, communicate, and join communities to build Visibility.
- Consistency is key: Accept failing multiple interviews, treat it as a normal process, and keep improving instead of giving up.
- The real-world working mindset always revolves around 3 circles: Passion, Responsibility, and Benefits. Sometimes you have to do things you dislike because it falls under "Responsibility".

#### Architecture & Technical Skills
- Future engineers will no longer focus on writing thousands of lines of code but will shift to **Architecture Thinking** – designing system architectures and integrating existing services.
- AI cannot replace an engineer's ability to deeply understand Business Problems and Design Architectures.
- Use standard frameworks (like AWS Well-Architected) to self-assess and review personal projects to elevate them to Production standards.

### Application to Work & Study
- **Applying AI:** Use AI (like Amazon Q) to scan current projects. Prompt AI to act as a recruiter to point out vulnerabilities (Security, Scalability) for improvement.
- **Building Practical Projects:** Upcoming personal projects will not stop at just the UI but will solve pain points in specific domains (e.g., E-commerce, Fintech), incorporating the mindset of handling system "crashes".
- **Changing Communication Style:** Actively connect with teachers and friends, asking deep questions ("Why?"). Practice small talk to get used to expanding professional networks.
- **Focusing on Core Foundations:** Dedicate 100% effort to mastering foundational subjects like Data Structures, Databases, and Computer Networks.

### Experience during the Event
Participating in the AWS Study Tour was not just a technology exploration trip but a professional mindset awakening.

#### Reassessing the Competitive Landscape
- I realized the current market is "harsher" than I thought. Requirements for Interns or Juniors are no longer easy; companies need people who can work immediately (requiring knowledge of K8s, Cloud Native, AI).
- I saw the importance of having international certificates and a portfolio (blog, GitHub) to avoid being filtered out by automated AI CV screening systems.

#### Learning from Real-World Stories
- The failure and promotion stories from seniors working in startups to BigTech (ZaloPay, Heineken, AWS) helped me clearly understand the importance of balancing Technical and Business aspects.
- Ms. Nhu's story of persistently applying 11 times before getting into Amazon gave me immense motivation to never give up.

#### Lessons Learned
- AI was not created to make us lazier, but to force us to evolve into Seniors faster.
- Starting today, I need to change my learning approach: shift from studying just to pass exams to studying for deep foundational understanding, and actively build a relationship network for the future.
